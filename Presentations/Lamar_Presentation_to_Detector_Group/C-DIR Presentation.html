Fluorescence and Fluorescence Lifetime


  • Fluoresence phenomenon described by Jablonski.

  • Easily measured (CCD, EMCCD, Low Noise CMOS, by eye).

  • Fluorescnece Lifetime is the characteristic of this decay.

Fluorescence and Fluorescence Lifetime


  • τ is the fluorescence lifetime.

  • Not as simple/straightforward as intensity.

Importance of Fluorescence Lifetime


  • Used extensively as a microscopy within the life sciences. (FLIM)

  • Chemical environment of cells.

  • Describes interaction between fluorescent molecules (FRET used where ratiometric measurements are not possible).

  • Track chemical reactions/interactions at the subcellular level.

  • Used in cancer research as a non-invasive/non-destructive technique.

Capacitive Division

  1. 2D capacitor network. Measures capacitance at the intersections.
  2. Human fingers steal charge creating a change in the capacitance.
  3. This can be used to calculate the centroid of the disturbance.

The (Capacitive-Division Imaging Readout) C-DIR Anode

  1. Capactive anode not as sophisticated as mobile implementation
  2. Relative cheap and easy to manufacture using standard PCB techniquies.

The (Capacitive-Division Imaging Readout) C-DIR Anode

How it works

  1. Uses resistive sea PMT which contains dielectric substrate coated with a resistive layer.
  2. Resistive sea does not require vaccuum compatibility of the anode. Supports passive sensing.
  3. Localizes and capacitively couples charge onto C-DIR anode with predictable charge footprint.
  4. Charge is capacitively shared/divided between nodes due to the intrinsic board capacitances.
  5. Charge is propogates to the corner anodes where it can be read-out.
  6. Corner capcitances higher than internal capacitances (which are kept low to allow quasi-isotropic propoation).
  7. Higher perimeter capacitances improve linearity but produces a noise penalty on preamplifiers.

Charge Sensing (NINO ASIC)


  1. Preamplifier, pulse shaper and TOT discriminator.
  2. shaping amplifier (1ns peaking time), discriminator intrinisc noise < 10ps RMS jitter.
  3. Uses time walk to our advantage. Create lookup table.
  4. Calibration of time walk to charge in order to recover incident charge.

Time-Stamping Electronics (HPTDC)


  • CERN High Performance Time-to-Digital Converter (HPTDC).

  • Used 4 of 8 (up to 64) total Channels interfaced via LVDS (can use LVTTL).

  • FPGA buffers and packages timing packets.

  • Interfaced to PC using USB 2.0 (newer version uses USB 3).

 Example TDC packet.
  1. Allows configuration of time windows between 25ns and 100Us
  2. Each event will contain 10 32-bit packets. Lead and trail time for each of 4 channels, event header and event trailer.
  3. Software can then index into PW/Charge LUT and recover charge and hence charge centroid.

Data Reduction/Vizualisation

  • Sparse (Event Mode) data collection: x, y and t.

  • Reconstruction of intensity image.

  • Fast fitting to recover approximate τ (lifetime) with low stats.

  • Direct 2D image based on lifetime mapping.

  • Alternative phasor representation (frequency space transformation).
